The Tempi Railway Crash - February 28, 2023

Chronicle of Events & Official Records

Before The Crash

2014
September 26
Contract 717 for restoration of Signaling and Telecommand System is signed between ERGOSE (State owned infrastructure company) and a consortium (TOMI ABETE-Alstom Transport SA). The budget was 41.3 million euros and completion deadline was 2016. Supplementary Contract of 13.3 million euros was signed in 2021. The project remains unfinished to date. [1]
2017
2017
TRAINOSE (transport services company) is privatized and sold to Ferrovie Dello Stato Italiane S.p.A. (FSI). OSE remains a state entity responsible for infrastructure. [2]
2019
July 29
Telecommand System in Larissa is destroyed due to a fire but not restored. Thereafter, manual routing of the trains by the Stationmaster takes place. [3]
2021
September 06
Philippos Tsalidis, CEO of TRAINOSE, sends confidential letter to OSE and to the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport warning of the danger of a “severe incident” on the railway. [4]
September 14
Philippos Tsalidis resigns from the CEO position of TRAINOSE. [5]
2022
July
TRAINOSE is renamed Hellenic Train. [6]
2023
February 07
Democratic Unitary Trade Union Movement of Railways (DESK-S) issues a statement explicitly mentioning the possibility of an accident due to omissions in network safety measures. [7]
February 15
European Commission refers Greece to the Court of Justice of the EU for failure to comply with rules on Railway transport. [8]
February 20
During a speech in the Plentary of the Parliament, Minister of Infrastructure and Transport Kostas Karamanlis states that there are no safety concerns regarding the railway, dismissing the questioning of safety of the railways as “shameful”. [9]

The Crash

February 28, 2023
19:22
Passenger train IC62 of Hellenic Train, departs from Athens to Thessaloniki. [10]
21:40
Freight Train 63503 of Hellenic Train departs from Thessaloniki to OSE freight station in Athens. [11]
23:04
IC62, departs from Larissa station. [12]
23:06
IC62 enters the opposite line. [12]
23:21
IC62, after moving on the opposite lane for 17 minutes, collides head on with freight train 63503 near Tempi. Immediately, a massive explosion creates a fireball and fire spreads. [13][14]

Immediate Developments on the Accident Site

2023
March 1
Heavy machinery arrives at the site and helps with the aid Rescue Efforts. Removal of topsoil and debris takes place. [15]
March 2
The last 8 wagons of freight train 63503, which had not derailed, are sent back to Thessaloniki. [15]
March 2
Trucks begin to remove soil and debris from the accident site and move them to a designated area at the OSE depot in position Koulouri as well as in a private lot in position Pigadia. [16] [17]
March 3
Meeting between Regional Governor of Thessaly Kostas Agorastos, Deputy Minister to the Prime Minister Christos Triantopoulos, General Secretary of the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport Ioannis Xifiaras, General Secretary of the Ministry of Civil Protection, Vassilis Papageorgiou along with senior officers of the Hellenic Police and Fire Service and Representative of Hellenic Train takes place. They decided on the complete reconfiguration of the accident site citing the resumption of Railway Services. [18]
March 3
General Secretary of the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport Ioannis Xifiaras informs Hellenic Train executive that “the damaged rolling stock must be removed as soon as possible” so that at least one of the railway track could be delivered to OSE by the 6th of February. [15]
March 3
The Fire Service’s investigation on the cause of the fire is considered completed, and they depart from the accident site. [19]
March 4
The ground in the accident site is excavated in significant depth, filled with gravel and covered with concrete. [20]
March 4
The wreckage of the freight train’s second engine is moved to Koulouri without forensic examination. [18]
March 6
Reconfiguration of the accident site is concluded. [21]

Immediate Off-site Developments

2023
March 1
Stationmaster of Larissa Railway Station is arrested and charged with negligence. [13]
March 1
Minister of Infrastructure and Transport Kostas Karamanlis resigns. [22]
March 1
The CEOs of OSE (Spyros Pateras) and ERGOSE (Christos Vinis) resign. [23]
March 1
Massive demonstrations and strikes spread across the country lasting many days. [24][25]
March 1
Recordings between the Stationmaster and train driver of IC62 are leaked to the press. [26]

Further Developments (2023-2025)

2023
March 6
Investigators request the video footage of IC62 and the stations it stopped at from the Security company “Interstar” (responsible for the cameras in the Railway route). [27]
March 9
Charges are filed against two additional stationmasters and one inspector. [28]
March 11
Video footage from a mini market showing freight train 63503 passing through Leptokarya town is released to the press. [29]
May 21 &
June 25
Kostas Karamanlis is reelected as a Member of Parliament. [30][31]
June 2
Prosecutor Popi Papadopoulou of the European Public Prosecutor’s Office (EPPO) requests an investigation into former Ministers of Infrastructure and Transport Christos Spirtzis and Kostas Karamanlis. [32]
July 17
Following request by relatives of victims, Investigative Judge of Larissa District Court requests video footage of the loading freight train 63503. [27]
August 2
The Tempi Accident Victims Association Personnel serve an extrajudicial notice to Kostas Agorastos asking for explanation regarding the alteration of the accident site. [33]
August 4
OSE and Security Company "Interstar" deliver 6 hard drives which they claimed showed the full loading process in Thessaloniki Commercial Station. They state that the footage has been deleted and needs to be retrieved. [27]
November
Anubis Coldcase K9 Team investite the depot in Koulouri and finds the black box of IC62, human bone fragments belonging to victim, personal items of the victims, along with other debris (On March 1, 2023, the Fire Department had refuted the offer by Anubis to help). [34] [35] [36]
November 23
After the proposal of KKE and the approval of the majority of the Parliament, a Parliamentary Inquiry Committee titled “Committee of Inquiry for the investigation of the Tempi crime and all aspects related to it” is established. Nea Dimokratia (16 Members) holds the ultimate majority against the opposition (15 Members). [37] [38]
December 11
The European Public Prosecutor’s Office charges 23 non-political suspects with fraud and breach of trust regarding Contract 717. [39]
2024
February 5
The Larissa District Court Prosecutor files charges for misdemeanor against Kostas Agorastos for breaches of duty regarding the accident site alteration. [40]
February 20
Syriza, Pasok, Elliniki Lysi, Nea Aristera, Spartiates and KKE withdraw from the Parliamentary Inqury Committee denouncing it as a cover-up attempt. [41]
February 22
Philippos Tsalidis (Former CEO of TRAINOSE) testifies to the Interogator that former Minister of Infrastructure was aware of the railway’s condition. He confirms that Trains were not monitored in real-time (Telecommand System), there was no visible tracking of train movement per direction (Signaling), GSM-R (Emergency communication system) was nonfunctional and ETCS (ensures trains don’t exceed safe speed or distance, and stops them automatically if needed) was not active due to lack of a complete signaling system. [42]
February 28
1st Anniversary of the Accident. Massive Protests and Strikes take place. [43]
March 10
The Parliamentary Inquiry Committee concludes, with the majority approving the report issued by Nea Dimokratia, which attributes the crash to “human error” and Hellenic Train, stating that there was no cover-up. [44]
March 24
Newspaper “To Vima” reports that audio recordings of the stationmaster on the night of the crash were edited before leaked to the press, to reinforce the “human error” narrative. [45]
March 26
Pasok, Syriza, Nea Aristera and Plefsi Eleftherias file a motion of no confidence against the government which is rejected in the Plenary of the Parliament on March 28th. [46] [47]
April
Anubis Coldcase K9 Team investigates the private lot at Pigadia and finds personal items and bone fragments belonging to victims. [48]
November 11
Investigative Judge discovers that the submitted footage of, allegedly, the Thessaloniki Commercial Station showing the loading of freight train 63503 is actually from the Thessaloniki Passenger Station. The relevant footage is considered lost/overwritten. [27]
November 18
Supreme Court Prosecutor, Georgia Adeilini, orders urgent investigation in the reasons that the footage from the Thessaloniki Commercial Station showing the loading of the train was not delivered to the Larissa District Court. [49]
November 22
Interstar informs the investigator that there is video footage of the freight train passing from different points on the route. The footage is delivered on December 12. [27]
2025
January 20
An audio recording from the train right after the crash, where passenger is heard shouting “I have no oxygen” is published. [50]
January 26
Massive Protests with the slogan “I have no oxygen” take place in 110 cities in Greece and many cities worldwide. [51]
January 30
News report reveals that Police officers testified that they faced pressure from government officials to authorize the removal of debris and the reconfiguration of the accident site. [52]
February 1
The first civil court decision awards 800.000€ to a victim’s family as compensation assigning liability to OSE and Hellenic Train. [53]
February 1
News report reveals that the biological material recovered from the accident site was destroyed 40 days after the crash. [54]
February 5
Interstar lawyer, Vasilis Kapernaros, delivers to the investigator three new videos (in a USB stick) showing the freight train passing through Platamonas and Tempi tunnels the night of the crash. Two days later the videos are leaked to the press. [55]
February 10
Police Authorities raid the offices of Interstar with the purpose of seizing hard drives to verify the authenticity of the videos delivered by Vasilis Kapernaros. [55]
February 13
Court-appointed experts Apostolos Vasilakos and Stavros Mpatzopoulos analyzed the videos from Interstar and stated that no extra tank or 14th wagon were on the freight train though they could not certify the authenticity of the footage. [56]
February 28
2nd Anniversary of the Accident. Historically massive protests as well as strikes take place. [57]
March 3
Christos Triantopoulos resigns from the position of Deputy Minister for Climate Crisis and Civil Protection after allegations that he tampered the accident site. [58]
March 3
The Plenary of Parliament votes in favor of the establishment of a “Special Parliamentary Committee for the conduct of Preliminary Examination regarding the potential commission of the offense of breach of duty” by Christos Triantopoulos. [59]
March 18
Establishment of the Special Parliamentary Committee for the conduct of Preliminary Examination regarding the potential commission of the offense of breach of duty (Nea Dimokratia: 14 members, Opposition: 13 members). [60]
March 18
Christos Triantopoulos requests to be referred directly to the Judicial Council, by passing the parliamentary investigation process. [61]
March 28
Police forensics ruled the videos footage of the freight train from Interstar as genuine. [62]
April 7
The “Special Parliamentary Committee for the conduct of Preliminary Examination regarding the potential commission of the offense of breach of duty” concludes with the majority approving the proposal by Nea Dimokratia, for prosecution of Christos Triantopoulos for breach of duty. Opposition parties refuse to participate in the vote.[1] [63]
April 9
The Deputy President of the board of the Hellenic Air & Rail safety Investigation Authority Christos Papadimitriou (HARSIA/ EODASAAM) resigns as a result of the fireball section controversy. [64]
April 11
The Plenary of the Parliament approves the Proposal made by the Special Parliamentary Committee for the conduct of Preliminary Examination regarding the potential commission of the offense of breach of duty and refers Christos Triantopoulos to the Judicial Council. [65]
April 30
The case file regarding the former Ministers of Infrastructure and Trasport Christos Spirtzis and Kostas Karamanlis is transmitted to the Parliament. [66]
May 19
The Plenary of the Parliament votes in favor of forming “Special Preliminary Committee for the Conduct of a Preliminary Examination regarding ‘the tragic accident that took place on 28/02/2023 in the area of Evangelismos, Tempi, Larissa’” in which potential offenses for breach of duty by Kostas Karamanlis will be examined. [67]
June 25
The Special Preliminary Committee for the Conduct of a Preliminary Examination regarding ‘the tragic accident that took place on 28/02/2023 in the area of Evangelismos, Tempi, Larissa’ is established (Nea Dimokratia: 14 members, Opposition: 12 Members). [68]
June 26
The Committee’s majority rejects the opposition’s list of witnesses, refuses to grant access to the full case file and calls only Kostas Karamanlis as a witness to testify. [69]
July 7
Kostas Karamanlis, instead of showing up to the Committee, sends memo in which he denies committing any criminal act but expresses his desire to be referred to the Judicial Council to prove his innocence. [70]
July 17
The Special Preliminary Committee for the Conduct of a Preliminary Examination regarding ‘the tragic accident that took place on 28/02/2023 in the area of Evangelismos, Tempi, Larissa’ concludes with the majority approving the proposal by Nea Dimokratia, for prosecution of Kostas Karamanlis for breach of duty. Opposition parties refuse to participate in the vote. [71]
July 22
The Plenary of the Parliament votes in favor of the Proposal made by Special Preliminary Committee for the Conduct of a Preliminary Examination regarding ‘the tragic accident that took place on 28/02/2023 in the area of Evangelismos, Tempi, Larissa’ and refers Kostas Karamanlis to the Judicial Council. [72]

Technical Reports

2023
March 1
Report: Report by the Fire Service (1)
Findings: The fire was caused by the collision of the two trains. [73]
April 20
Report: Report by the "Committee of Experts" by the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport
Findings: Accident is a result of chronic systemic pathogens and human error. [12] [74]
May 10
Report: Technical report by General Chemical State Laboratory (1)
Findings: Analysis of soil and debris samples. According to the report traces of sylicon oil, xylene, toluene and benzene were detected. [75]
June
Report: Judicial Expert Report
Findings: Attributes the cause of the crash to human error of the stationmaster as well as the train driver of the passanger train while noting that safety systems would have prevented the accident. Attributes the fire to electric arcs and the fireball to silicon oil. [76]
July 6
Report: Report by the Fire Service (Supplementary) (2)
Findings: The fireball was caused by the ignition of silicon oil from the the locomotive transformers. [73]
July 17
Report: EDAPO (Committee of Independent Experts of the Victims Families) (1)
Findings: The lack of safety systems, human error and deficiencies in personel training contributed to the accident while the fireball is atributed to an "unknown combustion matter", citing the detection of xyline. [77]
December 1
Report: Kakamoukas & Kakamoukas - Hired by the victim's family.
Findings: The collision was caused by a combination of human error and lack of safety systems. Attributed fire to the collision of the passanger train with high voltage overhead tables, and the fireball to silicone oil. [78]
2024
January
Report: Anastasios Dedes - Technical Advisor Report- hired by victim's family
Findings: The fire and the fireball was caused by 7 tons and 200 kilos of silicon oil. [79]
February 2
Report: Technical report by General Chemical State Laboratory (2)
Findings: Analysis in soil samples from the accident site. According to the report xylene, toluene and benzene have were detected. [41]
February 16
Report: Paul Jowett (Burgoynes) - Hired by victims' family
Findings: Concluded the fire was likely caused by silicone oil from transformers, though other materials couldn't be ruled out. It notes that the accident site was altered before the collection of the samples for chemical analysis. [80]
February 23
Report: Technical Advisor Vassilis Kokotsakis appointed by victims' families
Findings: Xylene, Toluene and Benzene were detected albeit they should not have been present. Rejects the theory that the fireball was caused by silicon oil and argues that it was caused by a large quantity of chemical solvents. [81]
February 26
Report:EDAPO (Committee of Independent Experts of the Victims Families) (2)
Findings: Mapped every victims' and injured passanger's position. Concluded that at least 5-7 victims survived the collision but died due to the fire. [82]
May 16
Report: Technical Advisor Vassilis Kokotsakis appointed by victims' families
Findings: The crash was not a head-on collision; The two electric locomotives of the fright train derailed moments before the crash. The fireball was caused by a large load of undecleared chemicals which is estimated to be approximatelly 12-15 tons. Rejects the theory that the fireball was caused by silicon oil. The accident site was systemically altered and timeline of the alteration is provided. [75]
2025
January 12 &
January 30
Report: Technical Advisor Vassilis Kokotsakis appointed by victims' families
Findings: Argues that the accident site was altered while reporting the presense unidentified individuals who were not part of the rescue teams. Also, concludes that 27 victims survived the collision but died due to the fire. [83] [84]
February 22
Report: Yves Carton (on behalf of Hellenic Train)
Findings: Rejects the presence of illegal cargo and concludes that the fire and the fireball was a result of "unforunate coincidents" stating that the the fire and fireball were results of Silicon oil and electric arcs. [85]
February 27
Report: Technical report by "The Hellenic Air & Rail safety Investigation Authority (HARSIA/ EODASAAM)"
Findings: Initially concluded that the crash was caused by systemic abandonment of the railway while the fire and fireball could not have been caused by silicon oil, pointing to “unknown fuels” (hydrocarbon) siting simulations by the Univesities of Ghent and Pisa. Also noted that evidence was lost due to alteration of the accident site.[1] On April 7, after, both Universities objected to how the data was used, the section regarding the fireball was removed from the report. [11] [86]
April 30
Report: Thechnical report of Derbis inspection by the National Technical University of Athens (Prof. P Tsakiridis)
Findings: Examined 9 metal fragments collected from the rear of drivers' of the 2nd electric locomotive of the freight train and determine that the damage on them was caused by mechanical stress not by fire or explosion. [87]
May 8
Report: Thechnical report regarding the fireball by the National Technical University of Athens (Prof. D Karonis)
Findings:The fireball was likely caused by electric arcs and silicon oil breaking into flamable gases(hydrogen/methane). The traces of Xylene, Toluene and Benzene could be products of thermal degradation of the trains construction matterials. [88]
May 27
Report: EDAPO (Committee of Independent Experts of the Victims Families) (3)
Findings:Detailed documentation on how the accident site was altered and concluded that the site alteration made it difficult to collect essential evidence regarding the fire and the victims. [89]
July 18
Report: Technical advisors Vasilis Kokotsakis and Federico Karasko
Findings: Before, during and after the seizure of Interstar Hard Drives on February 10th mass deletion of files takes place. Also, they report the existence of at least three more videos while pointing contradictions between them and the videos submitted by Vasilis Kapernaros. [90]
August 28
Report: Fire Service (Arson Crimes) (3)
Findings: Inspection of the wreckage at Koulouri on March 2025. According to the report no traces of fire on the second wagon of the freight train were detected. [91]
August 29
Report: Technical report by General Chemical State Laboratory (3)
Findings:Analysis in samples taken from OSE Depot in Koulouri. Xylene and toluene was detected. [92]
